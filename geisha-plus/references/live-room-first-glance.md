# Live-Room First-Glance Judgment

## Core Judgment

First-glance live-room judgment is not pure taste. It is audience-scene fit.

The question is not "is this live room beautiful?" The question is:

> Does the person I want to keep feel that this scene belongs to them, or belongs to the life they want?

## Five-Second Check

In the first five seconds, a viewer should roughly know:

1. Who this room is for.
2. What kind of product or value is being sold.
3. Why the room is trustworthy.
4. What emotional state or life scene it represents.
5. Why they should stay one more minute.

If these cannot be felt visually, the live room relies too much on the host's explanation.

## Audience First

Always start with the target audience:

- If the room wants to keep college students or people who just started working, the scene should usually feel bright, clean, simple, easy to enter, and brand-toned.
- If the room wants mature middle-class buyers, the scene may need more trust, order, material detail, and price justification.
- If the room wants gift buyers, the scene needs certainty, ceremony, packaging, and "not making mistakes".
- If the room wants price-sensitive buyers, the scene needs direct proof, quantity, comparison, and deal clarity.

Do not design a room only from the brand's preferred aesthetic. Design it from the audience's willingness to stay.

## Combine With Live-Room Logic

Use the "任意时刻" principle:

Live-room users enter at any moment. Visual information is the only layer that continuously exists. Therefore, the scene must carry stable information even when the host is silent.

Check four layers:

1. Person: does the host look like someone this audience trusts, envies, or accepts?
2. Product: can the audience see the product's category, quality, use, or quantity?
3. Scene: does the room prove identity, atmosphere, or consumption desire?
4. Content: is the repeated explanation supported by objects, actions, comparisons, and visual proof?

## First-Glance Scoring

Score each item from 0-2:

- Audience fit: does the room look like it belongs to the target audience?
- Brand tone: is the visual language consistent with the brand?
- Category recognition: can viewers instantly know what is being sold?
- Trust proof: is there visible evidence, not only words?
- Stay reason: is there a reason to keep watching now?

Total:

- 8-10: strong first-glance fit.
- 5-7: usable but needs clearer proof or audience signal.
- 0-4: scene and audience are likely mismatched.

## Diagnosis Sentence

Use this shape:

> 这个直播间第一眼的问题不是好不好看，而是它没有告诉目标用户："这是给你看的。"

Or:

> 如果你的目标是刚毕业一两年的年轻人，这个场应该更明亮、更干净、更简单，并且让品牌调性一眼成立。

## Avoid

- Do not judge only by decoration style.
- Do not copy high-online rooms by surface form.
- Do not let the room look disconnected from short-video and brand content.
- Do not push price before scene trust is established.
- Do not assume a host can explain away a wrong scene.
