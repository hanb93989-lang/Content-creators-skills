# Image-Text Content

## Use Case

Use this when the user asks about Douyin图文,文字投稿,图文投稿, search-driven content, or how image-text content can support e-commerce conversion.

## Why E-Commerce Should Use Image-Text

Image-text content fits "看后搜" and active search behavior. It can connect user interest to purchase decision and shorten conversion path.

There is also a supply gap: compared with short-video saturation, high-quality image-text content that satisfies deeper information and search intent can still be scarce.

## Two Main Types

### 文字投稿

Text is the main content. The image works as background or carrier.

Use it to:

- Grab attention quickly.
- Deliver information clearly.
- Trigger comments, votes, saves, and discussion.

Common forms:

- Big text sentence
- List
- Question
- Choice question
- Help request
- Short opinion

### 图文投稿

Image and text both carry meaning. The image can be product, person, scene, or atmosphere. Text supplements the visual.

Use it for:

- Lifestyle atmosphere
- Product display
- Shopping decision
- Scene record
- Personal taste

## Heat-Oriented Directions

Two high-potential directions:

1. Interaction and discussion
   - Help request
   - Extreme choice question
   - Preview or spoiler
   - One-sentence opinion
   - Gossip or "small news" style
2. Practical help
   - Help me choose / compare
   - Lists and checklists
   - Shopping guides
   - Travel, moving, gifting, skin care, electronics, or food decisions

## Quality Standards

Good image-text content should satisfy:

- Authenticity: from real experience, purchase, desire, scene, thought, or perspective.
- Personalization: shows who "I" am, what taste I represent, how I speak, and what discussion I want.
- Accuracy: users should know within one second what this content helps them solve.
- Clarity: structure and sentences should be easy to follow.
- Practicality: after reading, users can avoid mistakes, save effort, choose better, or act immediately.

## Diagnosis Questions

- Is this a text-led post or image-led post?
- Does the first page make the user know what this helps with?
- Is there a real "I" behind it, or does it feel copied?
- Does it invite discussion or reduce decision cost?
- Can it capture search demand after the user sees it?
