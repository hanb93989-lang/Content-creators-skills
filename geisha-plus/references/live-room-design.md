# Live-Room Design

## Core View

A live room is not only a selling space. It is a continuous content scene where audience trust, product understanding, and transaction impulse are built in cycles.

## Diagnosis Order

1. Audience segment
2. First-glance audience-scene fit. For this, also read `live-room-first-glance.md`.
3. Product ladder
4. Host identity
5. Scene trust
6. Talk-track rhythm
7. Visual proof
8. Transaction reason
9. Retention and repeat-entry design

## Scene Design

The live room should make the product's value visible without relying only on explanation.

Check:

- What can the audience see immediately?
- Does the target audience feel this room belongs to them or to the life they want?
- What object proves the claim?
- What comparison creates trust?
- What repeated scene can become memory?
- What does the host's identity make more credible?

For scene research, do not stay inside the user's own category. Study classic high-online live rooms from other categories and migrate the mechanism, not the surface form. A useful scene case should usually satisfy at least one of:

- It has proven attention, such as stable online audience above 100.
- It is an industry classic.
- It solves a visible trust, atmosphere, interaction, or conversion problem.
- It can be transferred to another category during project research.

## Product Ladder

Clarify:

- Traffic product: why enter and stay?
- Trust product: why believe?
- Profit product: why buy now?
- Repurchase or relationship product: why return?

## Talk-Track Rhythm

Use cycles:

1. Catch: name a concrete audience situation.
2. Explain: translate product value into daily use.
3. Prove: show evidence, comparison, detail, or user feedback.
4. Push: give a transaction reason.
5. Reset: open the next audience entry point.

## Avoid

- Do not design only words; design visible proof.
- Do not push price before trust.
- Do not treat all viewers as one audience.
- Do not let the live room look disconnected from brand content.
- Do not copy another category's live-room surface. Extract why it works: person, product, scene, trust object, interaction rhythm, or transaction trigger.
