# Audience Segmentation

## Core Judgment

Do not treat user segmentation as a demographic label. A public user model is only the base map. Content strategy needs a concrete person reconstructed from real language, comments, questions, and buying concerns.

The useful judgment is:

> This is not only "Z世代", "精致妈妈", or "新锐白领". It should become a person I can recognize around me.

## Two-Layer Method

Use two layers together:

1. Public layer: use common market and platform models as a rough starting point, such as Douyin's eight consumer groups, A1-A5 crowd layers, age, city, income, life stage, and consumption mindset.
2. Evidence layer: require the user to provide the product, brand, target category, and real comments. Use comments to reverse-engineer the person.

Never stop at layer 1. A broad sociological definition is too rough for content. It cannot tell the creator what words to use, what scene to show, or what desire to trigger.

## Required Inputs

Ask for as many of these as possible:

- Product or service name.
- Brand positioning and price range.
- Keywords the audience would search, such as "马歇尔音箱", category words, use-scenario words, and competitor words.
- Douyin/Xiaohongshu comments under relevant content.
- Comments under the brand's own videos or product pages.
- After-sales questions, private messages, buyer questions, customer service logs, or live-room questions.
- Competitor comment sections and negative reviews.

If the user cannot provide all of them, start with 20-50 real comments from one search keyword.

## Comment Reverse-Engineering

Read comments for four things:

1. Desire: what kind of life, identity, result, or convenience is being imagined?
2. Barrier: what makes them hesitate, complain, compare, or ask questions?
3. Language: what words do they naturally use? Do not replace them with industry language too early.
4. Scene: where does this need appear? Home, dorm, workplace, commute, party, gift, first job, family, parenting, etc.

Then cluster the comments into usable content groups:

- Already wants it, just needs a reason to buy.
- Likes the image but has price or trust concerns.
- Does not know the product, but wants the life state behind it.
- Has a practical problem and is searching for a solution.
- Buys for identity, taste, status, or gifting.

## Combine With Existing Content Strategy Methods

Use these content strategy tools as support, not replacement:

- 张小阳: convert the group into one concrete person with life details.
- A1-A5: identify relationship depth. A1 knows you, A2 is attracted, A3 has interacted, A4 has bought, A5 advocates.
- A/B/C/D expansion: start from the most convertible group, then expand outward.
- "用户是多种需求的合集": the same person may buy for function in one scene and identity in another.

## Output Shape

When answering, use this shape:

1. "我先不把他叫成一个人群，我先把他还原成一个具体的人。"
2. Name the rough public layer.
3. Quote or summarize the comment evidence.
4. Reverse-engineer the concrete person.
5. Give the content implication: what to say, what scene to show, what not to say.

## Example Logic

If the product is a Marshall speaker:

- Do not only say "年轻白领" or "Z世代".
- Search "马歇尔音箱" on Xiaohongshu/Douyin.
- Read comments about decoration, sound quality, price, gift, apartment, coffee corner, and style.
- Reverse-engineer whether the buyer is buying sound, taste, room atmosphere, social display, or a gift.
- Then decide whether the content should show sound proof, room scene, aesthetic identity, or gifting certainty.

## Avoid

- Do not produce a user persona without real language evidence when evidence can be requested.
- Do not let "八大人群" become the final answer.
- Do not use only age/gender/city to decide content.
- Do not translate user language into brand jargon too early.
- Do not assume one product has only one user layer.
