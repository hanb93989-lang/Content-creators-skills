# Script Diagnosis

## Diagnosis Order

Review scripts in this order:

1. Purpose: what should this video achieve?
2. Speaking object: is this script facing consumers, brands, or a third-party observer? For word-level rewriting, also read `script-rewrite-standards.md`.
3. Audience: who is being spoken to?
4. Hook: why stop in the first seconds?
5. Information path: can the audience follow the logic?
6. Emotion path: does the feeling rise, turn, or land?
7. Visualization: can each sentence become a shot?
8. Transaction reason: what makes trust, inquiry, or purchase more likely?
9. Ending: what should remain in the audience's mind?

## What To Mark

When diagnosing, be specific:

- Which sentence is too abstract.
- Which word can be changed.
- Which paragraph has no visual action.
- Which claim lacks evidence.
- Which selling point has not become a scene.
- Which emotion is named but not made visible.

## Rewrite Rule

Do not rewrite the whole script immediately unless the user asks. First explain the main structural problem, then rewrite the highest-leverage section.

Before changing words, clarify the script's speaking object. A sentence that works for brand-side strategy may feel distant to consumers; a sentence that works for consumers may feel too informal for brand diagnosis.

## Common Problems

- The opening explains instead of creates a reason to watch.
- The script has information but no judgment.
- The script has emotion words but no emotional evidence.
- The product appears too late or too bluntly.
- The ending summarizes but does not leave an image.
- The hook names a selling point but does not trigger a scenario, pain,反差, or curiosity.
- The script lists features but does not show proof through a high-light shot.
- The user benefit is too broad, such as "好用", "舒服", "高级", and lacks a visible action.

## High-Light Diagnosis

For product scripts, mark three layers:

1. 高光话术: the sentence that creates stop, trust, curiosity, or purchase reason.
2. 高光镜头: the shot that proves the sentence.
3. 可视化卖点: the specific product value made visible by the shot.

If one layer is missing, diagnose it explicitly:

- Has话术 but no镜头: the audience hears a claim but cannot believe it.
- Has镜头 but no话术: the audience sees an object but does not know why it matters.
- Has卖点 but no场景: the audience understands the product but does not feel "this is for me".

## Output Shape

Use:

1. "我会先抓一个核心问题..."
2. "这条不是差在..., 而是差在..."
3. "最该先改的是..."
4. Show the before/after wording.
5. Ask whether to continue with structure, hook, or full rewrite.
