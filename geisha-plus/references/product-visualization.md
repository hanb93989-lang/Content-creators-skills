# Product Visualization

## Core View

Selling points are not content yet. A selling point becomes content only when it is translated into a scene, a comparison, a detail, or a user decision.

## Translation Path

Use:

1. Product function
2. User situation
3. Visible scene
4. Emotional or practical value
5. Transaction reason

Example:

- Function: quiet coffee machine.
- Scene: making coffee early without waking family.
- Value: private morning ritual and家庭边界感.
- Transaction reason: solves noise anxiety and upgrades daily life.

## Visualization Methods

- Show before and after.
- Show scale, texture, sound, weight, speed, or use detail.
- Put the product into a real生活场景.
- Use a person with clear taste or expertise as判断者.
- Show the decision process, not only the result.
- Use sensory translation: make smell, sound, touch, weight, freshness, softness, sharpness, breathability, comfort, and durability visible through action or comparison.
- Use stress tests when the product claim needs proof, such as scraping, pulling, dropping, squeezing, soaking, cutting, rubbing, or side-by-side comparison.
- Use a real场景 to connect product, person, and environment. If 出镜形象、场景/环境、商品 feel mismatched, the content loses trust.

## Diagnosis Questions

- Can this selling point be seen?
- If not, what object or action can prove it?
- Which audience segment cares about this most?
- Is the product solving function, emotion, or self-realization?
- What makes the audience believe this is not just广告话术?
- Is the scene rich enough: season, occasion, skin type, preference, usage habit, home environment, social situation, or purchase anxiety?
- Does the content show only product appearance, or does it show use, result, detail, and decision reason?

## Common Visualization Conversions

- Comfort -> close-up of wearing state, pressure marks, walking/running/long-use result.
- Breathability -> smoke, steam, water vapor, dry/wet contrast, sweat scenario.
- Freshness -> water droplets, cut surface, sound, juice, branch/leaf/origin detail.
- Texture -> strong side light, slow rotation, macro close-up, touch or rub.
- Durability -> scratch, pull, fold, wash, impact, repeated use.
- Storage convenience -> before/after mess, opening/closing action, capacity, cleaning path.
- Face/body shaping -> before/after on-body comparison, angle comparison, silhouette line.
- High-end feeling -> detail, origin, brand mark, ritual scene, restrained environment.

## Avoid

- Do not list selling points.
- Do not overuse adjectives.
- Do not say "高级", "好用", "有质感" without visual evidence.
- Do not detach the product from the user's life.
